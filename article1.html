Self-Assessment Tax Return Deadline — Are You Ready?

📅 January 2025 👁 3 min read ✍ Ali Mohamed

The 31st January deadline for online self-assessment tax returns is fast approaching. If you are one of the millions of people required to file a self-assessment return, now is the time to make sure everything is in order.

Who Needs to File a Self-Assessment Return?

You must file a self-assessment tax return if you:

  • Are self-employed as a sole trader and earned more than £1,000
  • Are a partner in a business partnership
  • Have income from renting out property
  • Have income from savings, investments or dividends
  • Earn over £100,000 per year
  • Received any foreign income

What Are the Penalties for Missing the Deadline?

Missing the 31st January deadline can result in automatic penalties from HMRC:

  • 1 day late — £100 fixed penalty
  • 3 months late — £10 per day (up to 90 days)
  • 6 months late — 5% of tax due or £300, whichever is greater
  • 12 months late — A further 5% or £300

What Do You Need to File?

To complete your self-assessment return you will need:

  • Your Unique Taxpayer Reference (UTR)
  • Your National Insurance number
  • Details of all income received during the tax year
  • Records of any expenses you wish to claim
  • P60 or P45 if you were employed
